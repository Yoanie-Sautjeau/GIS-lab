Land Cover Classification

For the third assignment, we explored various types of classifications. We utilized data from Google Earth Engine from Amsterdam in May and June 2024, importing it into ArcGIS. We generated different maps using different classification methods to determine the most suitable approach.

Unsupervised Pixel-Based Classification

This method segments the image into groups of pixels based on their spatial properties and classifies these segments into different categories without using predefined labeled data.

Unsupervised Object-Based Classification

This approach classifies individual pixels into categories based on their spectral properties without relying on labeled data.

Supervised Pixel-Based Classification

Using labeled training data, this method classifies individual pixels into predefined categories based on their spectral signatures.

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index

To monitor vegetation health, NDVI calculates vegetation health and coverage by comparing the difference between near-infrared and red light reflectance. Through these classifications, we observed differences in vegetation health between agricultural areas, protected natural parks, and the city center of Amsterdam.
