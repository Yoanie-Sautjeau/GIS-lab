Geographical Information System

Geographic Information System (GIS) is a framework to gather, manage, analyze and visualize geographical data. GIS integrates various types of data such as satellite imagery, maps, demographics, and environmental factors. The data are geographically referenced, meaning they are associated with locations on the Earth's surface. Their integration allows analysts to overlay, compare, and analyze data from various sources.

Maps generated through GIS can display complex information in a visually intuitive format, which is particularly useful in communication and decision-making. Additionally, GIS can be used to model and predict real-world scenarios based on spatial data analysis. By simulating scenarios and predicting outcomes, GIS aids in planning and forecasting future events and developments.

GIS finds applications in multiple fields such as Urban Planning and Development, Natural Resource Management, Disaster Management, Public Health, Environmental Analysis, and even Transportation and Logistics.
